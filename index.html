Vision

Create a regenerative destination to help people destress from daily life and reconnect with nature through short-term rentals, weekend retreats, and educational experiences. Guests explore scenic trails by day, enjoy spa services and farm-to-table meals by night, and connect with like-minded people under the stars.

This foundation grows into a regenerative agriculture food supply and a self-sustaining safe haven for future generations.

Values

  • Accountability: Take ownership of our impact on each other and the environment.
  • Community: Provide purpose and belonging to employees, guests, and neighbors.
  • Abundance: Share knowledge, resources, and space with surrounding communities.

Key Goals

  • Produce clean, non-toxic food through permaculture, aquaponics, and pasture-raised meats.
  • Help working-class individuals heal burnout and realign with nature.
  • Teach holistic healing through medicinal herbs, indigenous culture, yoga, and breathwork.
  • Promote self-sufficiency through DIY education.
  • Build a regenerative, self-sustaining haven for the future.

Location

  • 50+ acres near Hartford or Elkins, Arkansas
  • 10 minutes from forestry trail systems (hiking, horseback riding, off-road)
  • 15 minutes from gas stations and grocery stores
  • Accessible by car with no zoning restrictions
  • On-site water source (lake)
  • 1 hour from retail outlets for produce and farm goods sales

Amenities

  • Lodging: Glamping tents, tipis, covered wagons, A-frame cabins, treehouses, vintage campers, yurts, domes, train cabins
  • RV Sites: Full hookups with electric and water
  • Bathhouses: Indoor/outdoor showers, private spa bathhouses
  • Communal Areas: Outdoor cooking, chuckwagon area, community building
  • Wellness Facilities: Sweat lodge, spa, yoga, massage, meditation
  • Recreation: Nature-themed playground, tree nets, volleyball, hiking trails
  • Retail: General store, produce stand, farm-to-table restaurant (future)
  • Water Features: Natural swimming pond with waterfall

Activities

  • ATV & horseback riding
  • Hiking, swimming, fishing, hunting
  • Farm-to-table meals
  • Orchard and aquaponics tours
  • Yoga, breathwork, massages
  • Indigenous workshops & cultural events
  • Seasonal live entertainment
  • Campfires, outdoor cooking, stargazing
  • Themed weekends (night rides, yoga retreats, adult-only events, native dance workshops)

Development Plan

Year 1

  • Phase 1: Buy land, 5 glamping wall tents, 10 RV sites, 2 bathhouses, outdoor cooking & game area
  • Phase 2: 5 A-frame cabins, 10 additional RV sites, 2 bathhouses, community building, playground & tree nets, hot tubs

Year 2

  • Phase 1: 5 treehouses, 5 vintage campers
  • Phase 2: 10 tipis, sweat lodge, bathhouse, outdoor communal kitchen
  • Phase 3: Orchard and berry patch

Year 3

  • Phase 1: 2 aquaponic greenhouses, natural swimming pond with waterfall
  • Phase 2: 8 covered wagons, chuckwagon cooking area, bison, Highland cattle, horses

Future Plans

  • Farm-to-table restaurant
  • Barndominium event center
  • 2 additional aquaponic greenhouses
  • Yurts, domes, train cabins
  • Old West town theme area
  • Outfitting & guided fishing excursions
  • Long-term food storage & security systems
  • Sustainable safe haven during economic downturns
